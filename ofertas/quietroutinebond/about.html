About Quiet Routine

A small, independent journal about attention, pace, and the ordinary habits that shape a day — written by people who are still figuring most of this out themselves.

Why this exists

Quiet Routine began as a shared notebook between three people who kept noticing the same thing: some days felt like they had room in them, and most didn't, and the difference rarely came down to how much was on the calendar. It came down to smaller things — how the first hour was spent, whether one task got finished before the next began, whether there was any silence at all. We started writing those observations down. This site is what that notebook became.

We are not researchers and we don't claim to be. Where we mention a general pattern — like the idea that attention has a cost when it switches tasks, or that the body seems to move in waves through a day — we're summarizing widely discussed ideas in plain language, not reporting original findings. We link to nothing we haven't actually read, and we try to say "seems to" and "tends to" instead of pretending to certainty we don't have.

What we write about

Essays here sit somewhere between memoir and observation: a slow first hour, single-tasking, a quiet house, reading on paper, closing rituals, rest, sensory load, and the small transitions inside a day. We do not cover diet, weight, medical treatment, or supplements — those aren't topics we're qualified to write about, and they're not what this journal is for.

Our content policy

  • Educational, not medical. Nothing here is health, diagnostic, or treatment advice. We don't discuss illness, medication, or weight.
  • No sales pressure. We mention our own app, Stillhour, but we don't push it inside essays, and essays don't exist to sell anything.
  • Plain claims only. We avoid stating unverified numbers as fact and prefer "some people find" over guarantees.
  • Original writing. Every essay is written for this site. We credit any idea we borrow and link to its source when we can.
